# Form 5472 (Information Return of a 25% Foreign-Owned U.S. Corporation or a Foreign Corporation Engaged in a U.S. Trade or Business)

## 1. Identifying Information (Initial part)
Basic data of the entity filing this form:  
- Name of reporting corporation
- Address
- EIN
- Country of incorporation
- Principal business activity
- Total assets
- Tax year
- Type of filer
- 25% foreign-owned U.S. corporation
- Foreign corporation engaged in U.S. trade or business
- Final / Amended return (yes/no)

## 2. Part I – Reporting Corporation
Additional information about the reporting entity:  
- Date of incorporation
- Country of organization
- Type of entity (corporation / LLC treated as a corporation)
- Principal activity
- Accounting method (cash / accrual)

## 3. Part II – 25% Foreign Shareholder
This section reports the foreign owner (in a single-member LLC, the foreign owner):
- Name of foreign shareholder
- Address
- Country of residence
- Percentage of ownership
- Nature of ownership (direct / indirect)
- Principal business activity of shareholder

In a disregarded LLC, the foreign owner is considered a “related party” for 5472 purposes.

## 4. Part III – Related Party
List of related parties with whom transactions occurred:  
- Name
- Country
- Relationship to the entity (owner, affiliate, etc.)
- Type of entity

In many cases:
- Only the foreign owner appears
- If there is another related company, it must also be listed

## 5. Part IV – Monetary Transactions
This is the core of the form. Report annual totals, not individual invoices.

Income / inflows:
- Sales of inventory
- Rents received
- Royalties received
- Interest received
- Commissions
- Service fees received
- Other income

Expenses / outflows:
- Purchases of inventory
- Rents paid
- Royalties paid
- Interest paid
- Commissions paid
- Service fees paid
- Other amounts paid

Typical examples:

- Owner pays personal expenses → Other amounts paid
- Owner lends money to the LLC → Loans (interest/no interest)
- LLC pays owner expenses → Service fees / other

## 6. Part V – Non-Monetary Transactions
Transactions without direct cash, for example:  
- Contributions of capital in kind
- Free use of assets
- Transfer of intellectual property
- Debt forgiveness

## 7. Part VI – Additional Information
Control questions:  
- Were there ownership changes?
- Did the entity file accurate information?
- Were there other relevant transactions not detailed?

## 8. Signature
Signed by an authorized person from the entity (manager / officer).

## Critical Points (very important)
- ❗ Penalty starts at USD 25,000 per Form 5472 for failure to file or maintain required records, with additional penalties possible after IRS notice.
- Filed together with Form 1120 (pro forma) for a foreign-owned U.S. disregarded entity, or with Form 1120-F for a foreign corporation engaged in a U.S. trade or business.
- Does not report taxes, only information.
- In a foreign single-member LLC:
- There is always at least one reportable transaction (initial capitalization, expenses, payments, etc.).
